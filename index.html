It's modestly satisfying to cross off completed list items. It's about structure and order, setting and accomplishing goals, remembering to do something. Lists are borne of wandering minds. Once you make your list you can get back to the task in front of you. But don't forget your list when you're done, cuz that'll require a new list.

4 the Day is built on lists. Click the Song button and a random selection will appear. The song is derived from the top 100 songs of the 2000s, according to a survey conducted by Rolling Stone in 2012 (time for a new survey?!). Click Album and you get Rolling Stone's top 100 albums of the 2000s. Those surveyed were record industry types; conjures up "Have A Cigar" by Pink Floyd The band is just fantastic, that is really what I think, Oh, by the way, which one's Pink?, and musicians.

Ah, but which musicians? Click Artist and you get a selection from the 101 greatest visual artists of all time according to someone. If you click the anchor tag in the artist description, it'll open a new page and take you to the Wikipedia entry for that artist. Astro will take you to NASA's Astronomy Pic of the Day.

The number that precedes the selected song, album or artist, is it's ranking. "Hurt" sung by Johnny Cash and written by Trent Reznor Nine Inch Nails, is the 15th greatest song of the 2000s! It is an intimate version, and shows Cash's greatness.

I don't normally pay attention to trendy music. That's why these Rolling Stone lists are new for me. At some point, I'll create a blog for this site, so you can comment. In the meantime...

4 the Day challenge: listen to the song, the album, read up on the visual artist, spend a few minutes sketching one of their works, then check in with NASA. To boldly seek out inspiration, where others have been before.
